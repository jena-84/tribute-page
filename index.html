Sally Ride

The first American astronaut woman to travel into outer space

Sally's time line

  • 1951 -Born in Los Angeles California, U.S.
  • 1973 -She earned bachelor’s degrees in English and physics from Stanford University after showed great early promise as a tennis player.
  • 1978 -She was selected by the National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA) as one of six women astronaut candidates.She received a Ph.D.in astrophysics and began her training and evaluation courses that same year
  • 1979 -She completed her NASA training, obtained a pilot’s license, and became eligible for assignment as a U.S. space shuttle mission specialist.
  • 1982 -Ride married fellow astronaut Steven Hawley; they divorsed after 5 years
  • 1983 -She became the first American woman in space while rocketing into orbit aboard the shuttle orbiter Challenger. The shuttle mission lasted six days, during which time she helped deploy two communications satellites and carry out a variety of experiments.
  • 1984 -In October,She served on a second space mission aboard Challenger;the crew included another woman, Ride’s childhood friend Kathryn Sullivan, who became the first American woman to walk in space.
  • 1987 -Ride resigned from NASA,after flying two missions on the Space Shuttle Challenger.
  • 1989 -She became a professor of physics at the University of California, San Diego, and director of its California Space Institute (until 1996).
  • 1999-2000 -She held executive positions with Space.com, a Web site presenting space, astronomy, and technology content.
  • 2012 -She died (aged 61) in San Diego, California, U.S
  • 2013 -She was posthumously awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom.
